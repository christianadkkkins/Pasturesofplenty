## Introduction

A central problem in physiological monitoring is that clinically important deterioration is often easiest to recognize only after the dominant observable excursion is already underway. In the cardiac setting, this means that ischemic or pre-ischemic instability may become most obvious only when conventional waveform changes are already pronounced, even though the underlying dynamical system may have begun to reorganize earlier. The significance of an early-warning framework is therefore not merely that it might detect an event sooner, but that it offers a different physiological description of what "earlier" means: not a larger amplitude threshold crossed in advance, but a measurable loss of ordinary dynamical freedom before the overt excursion is complete. In the present work, we examine whether a low-dimensional O(d) geometric factorization can capture that transition by tracking beat-to-beat configuration rather than relying exclusively on conventional magnitude-based summaries. Updated cardiac results now make that claim more precise: the completed 86-record LTST cohort supports a reproducible low-lock or `loose` family, but does not support a single coherent `rigid` class, indicating that the clinically important signal is real while the high-stiffness population remains heterogeneous.

The broader significance of the solar-wind analysis is that it tests whether this geometric view is specific to cardiology or instead reflects a more general property of unstable systems. If related precursor geometry appears in heliophysical data before geomagnetic storm onset, then the method is less plausibly a cardiac-specific heuristic and more plausibly a reusable instability formalism. At the same time, the solar results make clear that reuse does not imply identical routing: the geometric kernel may transfer, while the dominant precursor channel remains domain dependent. We therefore frame the present preprint around a deliberately narrow claim. The goal is not to assert a universal alarm threshold across systems, but to evaluate whether the same compact geometric representation can reveal early structural change in more than one domain, with cardiac monitoring as the primary translational target and the solar analysis serving as a cross-domain test of physical generality.

## Discussion

With the updated data in hand, the strongest conclusion is more precise than it was at the pilot stage. The completed 86-record LTST cohort does not validate a universal three-way cardiac taxonomy; instead, it supports a narrower and stronger statement. Baseline projective geometry robustly separates a `loose` cardiac family from the remaining records, with the best one-vs-rest discrimination achieved by the baseline p95 of normalized temporal 3-cell volume (AUROC 0.738) and baseline p95 temporal area loss (AUROC 0.715), while the high-stiffness population remains heterogeneous and does not collapse into a single coherent `rigid` class. That is a meaningful result rather than a partial failure. It tells us that the geometry is already capturing a real physiological distinction, but that the unresolved biology now lies within the non-loose subset rather than in the absence of a signal.

The solar-wind results sharpen that interpretation. In the OMNI analysis, `poincare_b` and `gram_spread_sq` frequently moved before geomagnetic storm onset, indicating that the same style of geometric precursor can appear in a system with entirely different microscopic physics. What transfers across domains is therefore not a single threshold or a fixed route to alarm, but a more modest structural principle: complex systems often reveal impending reorganization by first losing geometric freedom. A physically consistent interpretation is that the observed rate of manifold collapse is not arbitrary, but is bounded by the propagation velocity of the underlying potential field of the system. Under that view, the precursor geometry appears before the dominant excursion because the field reorganizes before the macroscopic signature is complete. The earlier 5-record LTST pilot was useful in showing that cardiac heterogeneity existed; the completed cohort now clarifies the stable claim that follows from it, namely that geometric early warning is real, phenotype dependent, and not fully exhausted by a single high-stiffness class boundary.

The completed detector runs now add operational context to that structural claim. In the onset-gated LTST transition-HMM run with a 32-beat cooldown, HMM alerts were visible before 7524/8000 ST episodes (94.1%) with median lead 429 beats, but beat-level lead-aware specificity was 0.671 and alert occupancy remained 0.335. In the broad full-archive solar layered run, `solar_hmm` moved before 546/589 storms with median lead 279.5 minutes, while minute-level specificity was 0.756, sensitivity 0.363, precision 0.235, and alert occupancy 0.265. A newer 2007-2008 slice then clarified the role of the solar HMM rather than simply improving its headline recall: in that sparse operating regime, `solar_hmm` moved before 19/29 storms with specificity 0.984, precision 0.132, and alert occupancy 0.017, while the geometric lead features remained much broader (`poincare_b` before 28/29 storms and `gram_spread_sq` before 23/29). Taken together, these runs suggest that the geometry itself is the more general precursor substrate, while the HMM is better understood as a tunable episode layer whose acceptable operating point depends on domain-specific alert burden.

An anchored-state beta sweep on that same 2007-2008 solar slice then provided a useful falsification. The best open-ranked pair (`beta_short = 0.2`, `beta_long = 0.003`) increased the number of storms for which `solar_hmm` was the earliest combined lead from 5/29 to 9/29, but did so only by reverting to a broad noisy regime with specificity 0.677, precision 0.060, and alert occupancy 0.329. Post hoc episode mining showed that the false-positive burden was not dominated by sub-hour chatter under the existing 60-minute cooldown; instead, 43.6%, 67.0%, and 89.8% of false-positive minutes occurred in episodes that began within 2, 3, and 6 hours of the previous alert, respectively. This result argues against beta tuning alone as the path to a useful middle operating point and instead points toward transition-only opening and materially longer refractory or cooldown logic as the more plausible next correction.

A second update strengthens the mathematical side of the argument. The newly added oriented/sheaf sidecar was backfilled across the full 86-record LTST cohort, and the theorem-facing evaluation showed that the discrete closure-defect approximation is not merely qualitative: across 8.82 million finite beat rows, the measured and predicted defect terms tracked one another with `phi_corr = 0.983`, `phi_mae = 0.0093`, and defect-ratio correlation `0.899`. This does not convert the proof sketches into exact equivalence theorems, but it does materially strengthen the claim that the oriented projective defect is measuring a real geometric quantity already present in the data rather than a decorative post hoc construction.

The second-order residual pass then tightened that claim in the regime where the refined theorem is actually meant to apply. Across `4,217,092` sign-coherent nondegenerate rows, adding the explicit quadratic term reduced `phi_mae` from `0.009304` to `0.005889`, a `36.7%` improvement. More importantly, the residuals followed the predicted shape law: `corr(|r_1| / theta_sl^2, 0.5 |cot b| sin^2 A_l) = 0.919`, and in the near-coplanar slice `min(A_l, pi - A_l) <= 0.05` the first-order defect MAE collapsed to `0.000670`. On the intended small-angle nondegenerate slice (`sin b >= 0.1`, `|theta_sl| <= 0.04`), the second-order correction reduced `phi_mae` from `7.33e-4` to `6.63e-5` and `phi_rmse` from `1.22e-3` to `1.35e-4`. The remaining large-RMSE tail on the unrestricted corpus is therefore consistent with the theorem's explicit `1 / sin^3 b` remainder scaling rather than unexplained model failure.

Outside the core cardiac and solar comparison, an exploratory Sleep-EDF benchmark also suggests that the same causal geometric machinery extends to neural regime transitions. On a single scored overnight record, the current one-channel detector saw 34/52 labeled sleep-stage transitions in advance with specificity 0.912 and precision 0.463. Because that experiment used only a single EEG channel rather than a richer anchored physiologic state, we do not treat it as a headline detector claim here, but it supports the broader interpretation that the representation is not domain locked.

## Conclusion

In conclusion, the present preprint supports a constrained but defensible general claim: low-dimensional geometric invariants derived from an O(d) factorization capture early instability structure across multiple physical domains, but the mapping from those invariants to actionable alarms remains system dependent. The simplest reading is also the most useful one: as systems approach failure, they appear to surrender their ordinary degrees of freedom in a measurable order, and the invariants record that surrender before the largest excursion is fully visible. A natural physical interpretation is that the rate of manifold collapse is bounded by the propagation velocity of the system's underlying potential field, so that the geometry can begin to tighten before the final observable excursion has fully arrived. Updated cardiac results support this interpretation in a specific and limited form: they validate a reproducible `loose` baseline family while showing that the residual high-stiffness population is not yet adequately described by a single rigid class. The solar-wind analysis, in turn, shows that precursor geometry can emerge before the dominant disturbance signature in a second domain without reproducing the cardiac routing pattern, and the newer sparse solar HMM slice suggests that the decision layer may be best deployed as a high-specificity sidecar rather than as the primary precursor channel. The most appropriate interpretation is therefore not universal threshold invariance, but a reusable geometric kernel coupled to domain-conditioned routing.

This conclusion also defines the most credible translational path for the cardiac program. Rather than attempting to embed the full routing logic on device, the validated beat-level representation can be reduced to a compact primitive packet consisting of `poincare_b`, `drift_norm`, and `energy_asym`, while summary statistics, phenotype routing, and final decision logic remain host-side. Fixed-point emulation of this V1 packet preserved exemplar ordering with negligible error and essentially perfect monotone agreement across the benchmark records, indicating that the primary geometric signal is compatible with low-bandwidth embedded streaming even before a complete on-device router is justified. The engineering implication is therefore as economical as the mathematics itself: only a small set of primitive observables must cross the hardware boundary, provided that the richer interpretation remains offboard. In that sense, the preprint closes with a dual result, namely a cross-domain scientific claim about geometric precursor structure and a concrete engineering contract for testing whether the cardiac primitives remain faithful under embedded deployment constraints.

## Figure Captions

**Figure 1. Regime dissociation in the 5-record LTST pilot.**  
Dual-channel kernel analysis of the initial five LTST records showed that the cohort did not collapse to a single detection mode. Two records were `long_only` (`s20041`, `s20051`), one was `long_first` (`s20011`), one was `angle_only` (`s20021`), and one crossed both channels at the same horizon (`s20031`). This pilot result motivated the later full 86-record analysis, which confirmed a robust `loose` versus non-`loose` distinction but did not support a single coherent rigid class across the high-stiffness subset.

**Figure 2. Solar wind router alignment around geomagnetic storm onset.**  
Median baseline-normalized trajectories across 589 OMNI HRO2 storm events from 2002-2009 showed both `poincare_b` and `gram_spread_sq` shifting before the collapse in `SYM_H` at storm onset. In the completed layered run, `poincare_b` moved before storm onset in 555/589 events and `gram_spread_sq` in 489/589 events; relative to paired southward-`Bz` onsets, positive leads were observed in 424/589 and 385/589 events, respectively. The pooled alignment supports a solar precursor regime in which routing invariants often move before the main storm signature fully develops, even though southward `Bz` remains the dominant canonical cue on many events.
